Gigstrella is the practice rig I wanted before every run of shows: a setlist-first workspace where every song in the set can be marked so I can keep drilling the exact entrance or ending that keeps me up at night. It launches instantly in the browser and keeps my focus on solving one transition at a time.

You can load as many songs as you need for a gig, and each one remembers its own BPM mode, notes, and markers. The UI is local first, meaning no cloud necessary, just drop your songs in from your phone, tablet, or laptop and go! 

The dual AB and CD loops are the heart of the app. AB is perfect for the verse pickup or the chorus hit, while CD can live on the outro vamp or a tricky bombastic ending. Flip either loop on, drag the markers, and the player immediately jumps back into the phrase until your hands stop second-guessing.

Because loop states, tempo modes, and notes are saved per song, you can bounce between tracks without losing the exact bar counts you dialed in. The next time you open Gigstrella, every region is waiting—no scavenger hunt, no spreadsheet.

And since it's 100% web-based, it works wherever you can open a browser: on the subway to rehearsal, in the back of an Uber on the way to the club, or backstage while the monitor engineer rings out wedges. If you have five spare minutes, you have enough time to tighten the spot that's giving you grief.
