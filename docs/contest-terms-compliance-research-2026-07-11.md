# StickWithIt Contest/Giveaway Terms Compliance Research

Date: 2026-07-11

Status: Not legal advice; attorney review recommended before launch or promotion.

Scope: Stick With It monthly drumming skill contest at `stickwithit.xyz`, related 24/7 stream/YouTube/social use of submitted videos, Discord community references, Fourthwall/merch context, and separate chance-based dashboard giveaways. This memo reviews the current draft at `https://stickwithit.xyz/contest/terms/` as fetched on 2026-07-11. External sources were fetched as untrusted reference material.

## Current Draft Snapshot

The live draft is short generic boilerplate. It states no purchase necessary, U.S. residents 18+, entry period listed on page, winner method may include public voting/judging/random selection/combination, odds depend on entries, prize ARV placeholder, a broad UGC display/publicity permission, release language, privacy language for administration/opt-in updates, and sponsor discretion to cancel/suspend/modify/disqualify.

Core issue: it combines a skill-based UGC contest and chance-based giveaways in one undifferentiated rules set, leaves ARV and sponsor identity incomplete, and does not yet address public-voting integrity, YouTube-required disclosures, COPPA/minor handling, AMOE mechanics for chance giveaways, state registration guardrails, tax documents, or music/third-party-rights constraints.

## Findings by Research Question

### 1. Skill Contest vs Sweepstakes vs Lottery

U.S. promotions are usually analyzed around three lottery elements: prize, chance, and consideration. A private promotion that has all three can be an illegal lottery. A sweepstakes keeps prize and chance but removes consideration through free entry/no purchase/AMOE; a skill contest keeps prize and may have consideration in some states but removes chance by selecting winners based on skill or merit under defined criteria. Olshan and Klein Moynihan both summarize the lottery triad this way; Promosis similarly distinguishes random winner selection from skill/merit judging.

Sources: `https://www.olshanlaw.com/sweepstakes-law-basics`, `https://kleinmoynihan.com/the-sweepstakes-amoe-in-a-nutshell-contest-marketing-lawyer/`, `https://www.promosis.com/promotion-compliance/skill-contest-vs-sweepstakes/`

For Stick With It, the monthly drumming contest is strongest as a skill contest if winners are determined by pre-announced skill criteria such as timing/groove, musicality, creativity, technical execution, and performance quality. The current draft's "public voting, judging, random selection, or a combination" clause is risky because random selection introduces chance, and public voting may reward popularity, vote campaigns, or bots rather than drumming skill.

Public voting is not automatically fatal, but it should be tightly structured. Recommended safer structures are: public voting selects finalists only; public voting is a fixed minority percentage of the final score; or public voting awards a separate "fan favorite" prize while judges select main winners. Promosis specifically warns that voting alone is higher risk and recommends clear voting rules, fraud controls, tie breakers, and sponsor/administrator final authority.

Sources: `https://www.promosis.com/contest-administration/public-voting-contests/`, `https://www.promosis.com/promotion-compliance/skill-contest-vs-sweepstakes/`

Free entry helps remove consideration from either structure, but it does not by itself make a random winner selection into a skill contest. For the monthly UGC contest, free entry is still good; the important legal classification point is that the winner should not be chosen by chance. For separate giveaways, random selection is acceptable only as a sweepstakes/giveaway with no purchase necessary and a compliant free entry path.

### 2. Sweepstakes / Chance Giveaways

Chance-based giveaways need clear official rules. Common required/recommended content includes: no purchase necessary; purchase does not improve odds; sponsor name/address; eligibility; start/end dates; entry methods, including AMOE if any purchase or consideration path exists; entry limits; prize description and ARV; odds disclosure; winner selection/notification; void where prohibited; tax responsibility; liability/publicity releases; and privacy/data use. Olshan lists these official-rules elements and says rules cannot change once a sweepstakes has begun.

Sources: `https://www.olshanlaw.com/sweepstakes-law-basics`, `https://kleinmoynihan.com/the-sweepstakes-amoe-in-a-nutshell-contest-marketing-lawyer/`

If the giveaway entry is only a free web form with no purchase, paid subscription, merch action, or burdensome non-monetary task, the "AMOE" is effectively the main entry method. If any purchase-linked action is added later, the rules and marketing should give equal clarity to the free AMOE, and AMOE entrants should have equal opportunity, equal odds, and the same prize availability as purchase-path entrants.

Source: `https://kleinmoynihan.com/the-sweepstakes-amoe-in-a-nutshell-contest-marketing-lawyer/`

Winner-list obligations vary. For registered NY/FL promotions, filing/providing winner information is specifically required. Even for small unregistered promotions, a simple "winners list available by request" clause is common best practice, subject to privacy/minor considerations.

Sources: `https://dos.ny.gov/games-chance-registration`, `https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0800-0899/0849/Sections/0849.094.html`, `https://www.olshanlaw.com/sweepstakes-law-basics`

### 3. State Registration / Bonding

Florida: Florida Statutes Section 849.094 defines game promotions involving chance and prize in connection with consumer products/services. If total announced prize value is greater than $5,000, the operator must file rules and prize categories at least 7 days before commencement and establish a trust account or surety bond for the total prize value. Florida also requires a certified winners list for prizes over $25 within 60 days after winners are determined, and the filed rules may not thereafter be changed.

Source: `https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0800-0899/0849/Sections/0849.094.html`

New York: NY Department of State says games of chance used to promote consumer products/services with total prize value in excess of $5,000 must file a registration setting out rules, odds, and contest information, attach a certificate of deposit or surety bond for the total prize amount, pay a $100 fee, and file a certification of winners within 90 days after completion for prizes over $25. N.Y. GBL Section 369-e similarly requires filing at least 30 days before commencement when the total announced prize value exceeds $5,000.

Sources: `https://dos.ny.gov/games-chance-registration`, `https://newyork.public.law/laws/n.y._general_business_law_section_369-e`

Rhode Island: R.I. Gen. Laws Section 11-50-1 applies to a "retail establishment" offering chance-based prizes to promote its retail business when total announced value exceeds $500, requiring a filing with a $150 fee. Rhode Island's broader games-of-chance regulations are charity/organization oriented and state that games of chance must be authorized, with internet restrictions for licensed games. For Stick With It, the Rhode Island point is a caution because Stick With It has a merch shop; attorney should decide whether online creator/merch giveaways implicate the retail rule. Keeping any individual chance giveaway under $500 ARV and not tying it to a retail-store purchase helps reduce the issue.

Sources: `https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/statutes/title11/11-50/11-50-1.HTM`, `https://rules.sos.ri.gov/regulations/Part/270-50-00-1`, `https://www.olshanlaw.com/sweepstakes-law-basics`

Small merch giveaways appear safely under NY/FL thresholds if each promotion's total announced prize pool is well below $5,000. Current visible examples appear low value: monthly contest prizes around headphones/sticks/stickers and a separate headphones giveaway. Recommended practice: track ARV per promotion and in aggregate, cap any single active chance promotion below $500 if Rhode Island is included until counsel reviews, and never let dashboard-managed giveaways accidentally aggregate into a >$5,000 active campaign without registration review.

### 4. Minors / COPPA

Current draft eligibility is 18+ only, which avoids most minor-consent and COPPA issues for entries, though the broader audience may include minors. If Stick With It wants to allow entrants under 18, the rules should either permit only ages 13-17 with parent/guardian consent or build a COPPA-compliant process for under-13 entrants. The FTC COPPA FAQ states COPPA applies to commercial websites/services directed to children under 13 that collect personal information, and to general-audience sites with actual knowledge they are collecting, using, or disclosing personal information from children under 13. Personal information includes first/last name, online contact information, screen name functioning as contact information, persistent identifiers, and a photograph/video/audio file containing a child's image or voice. The FAQ says COPPA applies even if the child voluntarily provides the information.

Sources: `https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/complying-coppa-frequently-asked-questions`, `https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/rules/childrens-online-privacy-protection-rule-coppa`

Because Stick With It collects name/contact and UGC video through a submission form and has webchat, the safest default is 18+ for both contest and giveaways. A workable alternative is 13+ with a parent/legal guardian checkbox and release for 13-17, and "under 13 not eligible" unless counsel builds verifiable parental consent, direct parent notice, parental access/deletion rights, child privacy policy language, and data minimization/retention controls.

Discord separately requires users to be at least 13 and meet the minimum age in their country, and says parent/legal guardian agrees to be bound for minors. If contest activity happens in Discord, do not invite under-13 participation through Discord.

Source: `https://discord.com/terms`

### 5. UGC Rights / Publicity / Music

YouTube contest policy says a YouTube contest cannot ask viewers to give all rights to, or transfer ownership of, their entry. Therefore Stick With It should use a non-exclusive license, not an assignment. The current draft's permission language is directionally right but should be clearer: entrant retains ownership; sponsor receives a worldwide, royalty-free, sublicensable, non-exclusive license to host, reproduce, edit for formatting, publicly display/perform, stream, distribute, and promote entries on the site, 24/7 stream, YouTube, social channels, and marketing in connection with Stick With It and the promotion.

Source: `https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1620498?hl=en`

Publicity rights should cover name, handle, likeness, voice, image, biographical information, and performance in connection with the entry and promotion, "where permitted by law." For minors, the parent/guardian should grant publicity/license rights. If the entry includes other people, visible third-party content, brands/logos, or copyrighted material, entrant should warrant they have permissions and sponsor should reserve takedown/disqualification rights.

The music angle is favorable if entrants drum only over the sponsor-provided track and Stick With It controls the track. The rules should grant entrants a limited license to use the provided track solely to create and submit/post an entry for the promotion, and should forbid unrelated copyrighted music, covers, samples, backing tracks, or third-party compositions unless entrant has all needed rights. Covers/other underlying compositions can create synchronization/master/public-performance/platform claim issues, especially if rebroadcast on YouTube or a 24/7 stream. Stick With It should moderate entries before stream/social use.

Source for platform/right restrictions: `https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1620498?hl=en`; UGC risk planning source: `https://www.promosis.com/contest-administration/public-voting-contests/`

### 6. Platform Policies

YouTube: All contests run on or using YouTube must comply with federal/state/local laws, YouTube Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Community Guidelines. YouTube requires official rules; entries not complying with Community Guidelines must be disqualified; the contest must be free to enter; metrics cannot be manipulated; YouTube cannot be associated/affiliated without prior written consent; rules must state YouTube is not a sponsor and require viewers to release YouTube from liability; and rules must include a legally compliant privacy notice explaining data use.

Source: `https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1620498?hl=en`

Discord: I did not find a Discord-specific contest/sweepstakes policy. Discord Terms and Community Guidelines still matter. Discord requires age 13+ and applicable minimum age, incorporates its Community Guidelines, and requires users to comply with laws. Community Guidelines prohibit spam, inauthentic engagement/metric manipulation, IP violations, illegal gambling, scams, and other illegal activity. Recommended rules: Discord is not a sponsor/administering platform, entrants release Discord, promotions must comply with Discord Terms/Guidelines, and no Discord spam/raids/metric manipulation.

Sources: `https://discord.com/terms`, `https://discord.com/guidelines`

### 7. Tax

IRS instructions require Form 1099-MISC for prizes and awards at the applicable reporting threshold; the practical promotion-law standard is to collect Form W-9 before awarding prizes valued at $600 or more and issue Form 1099-MISC when required. IRS W-9 requester instructions state Form W-9 is used by persons required to file information returns to obtain the payee's correct name and TIN, and the requester may rely on a properly completed signed Form W-9 to avoid backup withholding. Rules should state winners are responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes, and sponsor may require tax documentation before awarding a prize.

Sources: `https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1099mec`, `https://www.irs.gov/instructions/iw9`

### 8. FTC Endorsement / Testimonial / Dark Pattern Angles

FTC endorsement guidance says endorsements must be honest and not misleading, and material connections between an endorser and marketer that consumers would not expect should be disclosed clearly and conspicuously. FTC "Disclosures 101" says financial relationships include free/discounted products or other perks; disclosures should be hard to miss and placed with the endorsement; livestream disclosures should repeat periodically. The FTC FAQ specifically says even an entry in a sweepstakes or contest can be an incentive that may need disclosure if it could affect how people evaluate the endorsement.

Sources: `https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/disclosures-101-social-media-influencers`, `https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftcs-endorsement-guides-what-people-are-asking`, `https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-16/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-255`

For Stick With It: if entrants or winners post about the brand, merch, track, contest, or prize because of the contest/giveaway, rules should tell them to disclose the relationship clearly, such as "Contest entry for @StickWithIt" or "I won/received this from Stick With It." If Stick With It reposts winner testimonials as marketing, avoid implying typical results or independent reviews unless true.

Marketing opt-in: keep the "I agree to official rules" checkbox separate from any "send me updates/marketing" checkbox. The marketing checkbox should be optional, unchecked by default, and not required to enter or win. This reduces dark-pattern/consent risk and aligns with FTC expectations against hidden or manipulative consent interfaces.

Sources: `https://www.ftc.gov/reports/bringing-dark-patterns-light`, `https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/soliciting-paying-online-reviews-guide-marketers`, `https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-16/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-255`

### 9. Public-Voting Contest Specifics

Public voting should have its own section with: voting period, voter eligibility, one-vote-per-person/day/account/IP/device rule as applicable, whether entrants may vote for themselves, whether solicitation is allowed, prohibition on paid votes/bots/scripts/fake accounts/VPN/proxy abuse, sponsor right to remove suspicious votes and disqualify entries, sponsor/administrator final authority, tie-breaker, and technical-failure remedy. Consider hiding live vote totals or showing only rankings to reduce manipulation incentives.

Source: `https://www.promosis.com/contest-administration/public-voting-contests/`

Tie-breakers should avoid random drawings in a skill contest. Recommended: judges decide ties using a listed criterion, such as highest timing/musicality score, then highest creativity score, then panel decision.

Source: `https://www.promosis.com/contest-administration/public-voting-contests/`

### Stick With It Official Promotion Rules

Last updated: [DATE]

No purchase necessary to enter or win. A purchase will not increase your chances of winning. Void where prohibited or restricted by law.

These Official Promotion Rules apply to promotions offered by [LEGAL NAME OF SPONSOR] d/b/a Stick With It, [SPONSOR MAILING ADDRESS], [CITY, STATE ZIP], [CONTACT EMAIL] ("Sponsor," "Stick With It," "we," or "us"). Promotion-specific details posted on the applicable Stick With It contest or giveaway page, including entry period, prize description, approximate retail value ("ARV"), entry limits, judging criteria, voting period, and winner-selection method, are incorporated into these Official Promotion Rules.

These rules cover two types of promotions:

1. Monthly Skill Contest: a drumming performance contest in which entrants submit a video performance using the Sponsor-provided play-along track and winners are selected based on skill criteria and, if stated, public voting.
2. Chance Giveaways: separate random-draw giveaways for merchandise or other items listed on the contest page or dashboard.

If a promotion-specific page conflicts with these Official Promotion Rules, the more specific promotion terms control, unless prohibited by law.

### 1. Eligibility

Promotions are open only to legal residents of the 50 United States and D.C. who meet the age requirement selected below at the time of entry. Employees, contractors, officers, and agents of Sponsor, prize suppliers, promotion administrators, and their immediate family/household members are not eligible.

Choose one eligibility option before launch:

Option A - 18+ only: Entrants must be at least 18 years old at the time of entry.

Option B - 13+ with parent/guardian consent: Entrants must be at least 13 years old at the time of entry. Entrants under the age of majority in their state of residence must have permission from a parent or legal guardian to enter, grant the rights in these rules, and accept any prize. Sponsor may require the parent/legal guardian to sign an affidavit, release, and tax documents before awarding a prize.

Under-13 decision: Unless Sponsor implements a COPPA-compliant verifiable parental consent process approved by counsel, children under 13 are not eligible to enter and should not submit personal information, videos, audio, images, or chat messages through promotion forms.

Sponsor may require proof of eligibility. Void where prohibited or restricted.

### 2. Entry Periods

Each promotion begins and ends on the dates and times listed on the contest or giveaway page. All times are [TIME ZONE]. Entries received before the start time or after the end time are void. Sponsor's computer or promotion platform is the official timekeeping device.

### 3. Monthly Skill Contest: How to Enter

To enter the Monthly Skill Contest, during the posted submission period:

1. Download or access the Sponsor-provided play-along track listed for that month's contest.
2. Record an original video of yourself drumming or performing percussion to that track.
3. Submit the video through the Stick With It submission form at the URL listed on the contest page and provide the required contact information.
4. Agree to these Official Promotion Rules.

No purchase is required. Limit one Monthly Skill Contest entry per person per monthly contest unless the contest page states otherwise. Automated, bulk, incomplete, corrupted, late, or misdirected entries are void.

### 4. Monthly Skill Contest: Entry Requirements

Each entry must:

- Feature the entrant's own original drumming/percussion performance.
- Use only the Sponsor-provided play-along track, unless the contest page expressly allows other material.
- Avoid unlawful, hateful, harassing, sexually explicit, defamatory, dangerous, or otherwise inappropriate content.
- Avoid third-party copyrighted music, samples, cover songs, backing tracks, video clips, artwork, trademarks, logos, people, or private locations unless the entrant has all rights, licenses, consents, and releases needed for Sponsor to use the entry as described in these rules.
- Comply with applicable platform terms and community guidelines, including YouTube Community Guidelines if the entry is posted to or used on YouTube.

Sponsor may reject, remove, or disqualify entries that violate these rules, create legal/platform risk, contain third-party claims, or are inconsistent with the spirit of the promotion.

### 5. Sponsor Track Limited License to Entrants

For each Monthly Skill Contest, Sponsor grants eligible entrants a limited, revocable, non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Sponsor-provided play-along track solely to create, submit, and share an entry for that contest. Entrants may not sell, distribute, sample, remix, claim ownership of, or otherwise exploit the track except as expressly allowed by Sponsor in writing. This license does not cover any third-party music or composition not controlled by Sponsor.

### 6. Monthly Skill Contest: Winner Selection

The Monthly Skill Contest is intended to be a skill-based contest. Winners will not be selected by random drawing.

Before each contest opens, Sponsor will post the winner-selection method. Use one of these structures:

Option A - Judged winners, public fan-favorite optional: Judges select 1st/2nd/3rd place using the criteria below. Public voting may be used only for a separate fan-favorite recognition or prize.

Option B - Public voting selects finalists: Public voting identifies [NUMBER] finalists. Judges then select 1st/2nd/3rd place from the finalists using the criteria below.

Option C - Hybrid score: Public voting counts for [PERCENTAGE, recommended minority percentage] of the final score, and judges' scores count for [PERCENTAGE] of the final score.

Judging criteria unless the contest page states otherwise:

- Timing, groove, and musical fit: [__]%
- Technical execution and control: [__]%
- Creativity and interpretation: [__]%
- Overall performance quality: [__]%

Tie-breaker: Ties will be resolved by the highest score in timing/groove, then technical execution, then creativity, then final decision of the judging panel. Do not use random tie-breakers for the skill-contest prizes unless counsel approves a sweepstakes structure.

Judges' and Sponsor's decisions are final and binding in all matters relating to the Monthly Skill Contest.

### 7. Public Voting Rules

If public voting is used, the voting period, voting method, vote limit, and role of voting will be posted on the contest page. Unless the contest page states otherwise:

- Voting is limited to one vote per person per day.
- Sponsor may use email, account, device, IP address, CAPTCHA, rate limits, logs, and other reasonable methods to monitor voting integrity.
- Automated voting, vote buying, vote farming, bots, scripts, fake accounts, proxy/VPN abuse, coordinated manipulation, harassment, spam, or any attempt to impair voting integrity is prohibited.
- Sponsor may remove suspicious votes, hide vote totals, audit votes, require voter or entrant verification, disqualify entries, or modify/suspend voting if fraud, technical issues, platform issues, or abuse impair the integrity of the process.
- Entrants may share their entries and ask for votes, but they may not offer payment, prizes, reciprocal voting, or other consideration in exchange for votes.
- Sponsor's vote counts and integrity decisions are final.

### 8. Chance Giveaways: How to Enter

Chance Giveaways are separate from the Monthly Skill Contest. To enter a Chance Giveaway, follow the free entry instructions listed for that giveaway during the posted entry period and provide the required contact information.

No purchase is required. If Sponsor ever offers an entry path connected to a purchase, merchandise order, paid subscription, donation, or other consideration, Sponsor will also provide a free alternate method of entry with equal dignity, equal odds, and the same prize availability. A purchase will not increase your chances of winning.

Limit one entry per person per giveaway unless the giveaway page states otherwise. Automated, bulk, incomplete, corrupted, late, or misdirected entries are void.

### 9. Chance Giveaways: Winner Selection and Odds

Chance Giveaway winners will be selected in a random drawing from all eligible entries received for the applicable giveaway. Odds of winning depend on the number of eligible entries received. Potential winners are subject to eligibility verification and compliance with these rules before any prize is awarded.

### 10. Prizes and ARV

Prize descriptions and ARV will be posted for each promotion before entries open. Prizes are non-transferable, non-refundable, and may not be substituted or redeemed for cash except at Sponsor's sole discretion. Sponsor may substitute a prize of equal or greater value if the advertised prize becomes unavailable.
