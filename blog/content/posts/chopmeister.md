Chopmeister is a browser-based chop machine built for the moments before rehearsal when your brain wants inspiration but your hands don't have time to write it out. Open the page and you're already staring at a playable idea.

It's tuned for mobile from the jump. The buttons are thumb-sized, the sliders are forgiving, and you never have to smash an iPad with the butt of a stick or download yet another native app just to brainstorm a fill.

A random chop loads automatically. Tap the **Drums** button to spin another groove, or hit **Pattern** to reshuffle the sticking/voicing so the same rhythm lands on a new surface. It's a fast way to hear RLK/LKR inversions without opening a DAW.

The tempo and note-count controls live at the bottom so you can slow things down, stretch the subdivision, or stack more attacks before committing it to muscle memory. Now you don't have to throw change on the floor to come up with a linear idea that feels fresh.
