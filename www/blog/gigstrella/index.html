Gigstrella: Setlist Reps Anywhere

Published on Stick With It

Gigstrella is the way I keep a whole show in my back pocket. It opens to a setlist-first view so every song, loop, and note is already grouped by the moment it belongs to. When the van is parked outside the venue and I only have five minutes, I can still tighten the entrance that usually eats a quarter of rehearsal.

Add as many songs as you need for a night and each one hangs onto its own BPM mode, transport settings, and scribbled reminders. Upload the reference track once, slap the title on it, and Gigstrella remembers everything the next time you log in. No re-importing, no digging through folders.

The dual loop lanes are the secret sauce. AB and CD can live on completely different sections, so I keep AB on the verse pickup and CD on the outro hits. Drag the markers, flip the toggle, and it instantly cycles the trouble spot until your hands stop guessing.

Loop states, notes, and marker positions are saved per song, which means you can bounce between tunes without losing the exact two bars you dialed in yesterday. It behaves like a memory palace for your show—every transition is waiting right where you left it.

Because it’s a pure web app, it works anywhere there’s a browser: on the subway ride to rehearsal, riding shotgun on the way to soundcheck, or pacing backstage while the monitor engineer does their thing. Open the tab, punch the loop you want, and keep stacking confident reps while everyone else is doomscrolling.
