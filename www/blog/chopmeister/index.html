Chopmeister: Instant Linear Licks

Published on Stick With It

Chopmeister is a chill way to spark ideas before rehearsal without unpacking the laptop. Load the site and it instantly hands you a playable RLK sketch so you can get sticks on pads instead of staring at a blank notebook.

The layout is built for phones from the jump. Buttons are big, the controls respond to thumbs, and everything lives in the browser so you’re not downloading another app just to workshop fills.

A fresh chop appears the second the page loads. Tap the Drums button whenever you want another vibe, or hit Pattern to shuffle where the sticking lands so the same rhythm moves across different voices. Tempo and note-count controls sit right below so you can slow the idea down, lengthen it, or trim it to a tight four-count.

That loop keeps reheating every time you press the buttons, meaning you can chase variations while the band is talking through form changes. Now you don’t have to dump change on the floor for inspiration; you just tap the interface and let it feed new stickings.

Rig Notes

  • Five core voices — kick, snare, hats, rack tom, and a ghost-note lane — keep the grooves musical while still checking your dynamics.
  • Mobile-friendly UI with oversized transport controls makes one-thumb tweaking possible between takes.
  • Save a unique URL for every chop so you can text it to the band or revisit it later.

Launch it any time at stickwithit.xyz/apps/chopmeister.
