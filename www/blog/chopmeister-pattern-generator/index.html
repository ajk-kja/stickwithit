Chopmeister and the Pattern Generator DNA

Published February 24, 2026 · Practice log

I still remember killing time in a tour van with the old Android Pattern Generator app, poking at randomized cells until a fresh sticking fell out. Chopmeister is the browser version of that ritual. When a drummer has five minutes before tracking, they can open the grid, flip a few toggles, and let the loop run while tapping on a leg. No ego, no “look what I made,” just a low-stakes idea machine.

Why the workflow stuck

The charm of Pattern Generator was how quickly it rewarded curiosity. Chopmeister keeps that energy. Each lane still feels tactile on an iPad, so players can sketch with a stick butt or a fingertip. When something worth saving appears, the URL doubles as the share button—drop it in a band chat and the exact grid loads with zero logins.

How it shows up in real rehearsals

Take a halftime shuffle rehearsal: mute the kick lane, let snare plus hats drive the syncopation, and toggle probability on every other ghost note. Within seconds the loop nudges the drummer toward a fresher fill that sits better with the band. Once it lands, screenshot the grid for later and clear the board for the next idea.

Rig Notes

  • Four-lane matrix with velocity-aware playback so accents survive laptop speakers.
  • Every edit re-renders the URL parameters, making Discord or SMS sharing painless.
  • Probability sliders and future humanizer hooks live behind lightweight Web Audio nodes, keeping CPU low on tablets.
  • Local persistence via IndexedDB so half-finished chops survive a browser refresh mid-lesson.
